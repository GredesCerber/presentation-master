How Neural Networks Work

Architecture

Neural networks are inspired by the human brain:

  • Input Layer — receives raw data
  • Hidden Layers — process information
  • Output Layer — produces results
  • Weights — connection strengths
  • Activation — non-linear functions

Training Process

Networks learn through iteration:

1. Forward pass (prediction)
2. Calculate loss (error)
3. Backward pass (gradients)
4. Update weights
5. Repeat until convergence

Deep Learning Revolution

  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

    Excel at image and video recognition, powering face detection, medical imaging, and autonomous driving

  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)

    Process sequential data like text and speech, enabling translation and voice assistants

  • Transformers

    Power modern language models (GPT, BERT), revolutionizing NLP and generative AI

  • Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)

    Create realistic images, videos, and audio through competitive learning
