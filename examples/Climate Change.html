What is Climate Change?

"Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. Since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver, primarily due to burning fossil fuels."

— United Nations Climate Action

The Greenhouse Effect

How It Works

  • Solar radiation reaches Earth
  • Earth absorbs and re-emits heat
  • Greenhouse gases trap heat
  • Atmosphere warms like a greenhouse

Key Greenhouse Gases

  • CO₂ — Carbon dioxide (fossil fuels)
  • CH₄ — Methane (agriculture, landfills)
  • N₂O — Nitrous oxide (fertilizers)
  • CFCs — Chlorofluorocarbons (industry)

Historical Timeline

1896
Svante Arrhenius first calculates that CO₂ emissions could cause global warming
1958
Keeling Curve begins measuring atmospheric CO₂ at Mauna Loa
1988
IPCC established by the United Nations to assess climate science
2015
Paris Agreement — 195 nations commit to limit warming to 1.5°C
2023
Hottest year on record — global temperatures breach 1.4°C above pre-industrial
