Speech Notes

Climate Change — Global Environmental Challenge

Slide 1 ~30 sec
Title Slide

Good morning/afternoon. Today I'll be discussing one of the most pressing challenges facing humanity — climate change.

We'll examine the science, explore the impacts, and discuss what we can do about it.

Slide 2 ~40 sec
Presentation Outline

Our presentation is divided into two parts. First, we'll cover the science — what climate change is, how the greenhouse effect works, and the evidence.

Then we'll look at real-world impacts and potential solutions at global and individual levels.

Slide 3 ~1 min
What is Climate Change?

The UN defines climate change as long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. While natural factors play a role, since the 1800s human activities have been the main driver.

Three key manifestations: rising temperatures, shifting weather patterns, and more frequent extreme events.

Slide 4 ~1 min
The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is natural and necessary — without it, Earth would be too cold for life. The problem is human activities have intensified it.

Solar radiation reaches Earth, the surface absorbs and re-emits it as heat, and greenhouse gases trap that heat. The main culprits are CO2, methane, nitrous oxide, and CFCs.

Slide 5 ~1.5 min
Evidence of Climate Change

The evidence is overwhelming. Global temperatures have risen 1.1°C since 1880. CO2 concentration is at 420 parts per million — the highest in 800,000 years.

Sea levels are rising at 3.3mm per year. Arctic ice is declining 13% per decade. And oceans have absorbed 90% of the excess heat.

Key Numbers

  • +1.1°C temperature rise
  • 420 ppm CO2
  • 50% CO2 increase since pre-industrial
Slide 6 ~1.5 min
Historical Timeline

The science goes back over a century. In 1896, Svante Arrhenius first calculated that CO2 could cause warming.

In 1958, the famous Keeling Curve began measuring atmospheric CO2. The IPCC was established in 1988. The Paris Agreement in 2015 brought 195 nations together. And 2023 was the hottest year on record.

Slide 7 ~1.5 min
Environmental Impacts

The environmental impacts are wide-ranging. Oceans face rising levels, acidification, coral bleaching, and ecosystem shifts.

Land ecosystems see habitat loss, species extinction, and migration disruptions. Weather patterns bring more intense storms, longer droughts, heat waves, and flooding.

Slide 8 ~1 min
Human Impacts

Climate change directly affects human health and economics. Health risks include heat-related illness, poor air quality, spread of diseases, and food insecurity.

Economic costs include agricultural losses, infrastructure damage, higher insurance costs, and climate migration.

Slide 9 ~1 min
Carbon Emissions by Sector

Where do emissions come from? Energy dominates at 73% — electricity, heat, and transport. Agriculture contributes 12% through livestock and fertilizers.

Industry adds 6% from cement and steel production. Land use changes account for 6%, and waste contributes 3%.

Slide 10 ~1 min
Mitigation Strategies

Solutions exist. The renewable energy transition includes solar, wind, possibly nuclear, grid modernization, and storage solutions.

Carbon reduction strategies include EVs, building efficiency, carbon capture, and reforestation.

Slide 11 ~1 min
What Can We Do?

Individual actions matter. Transportation: use public transit, bike, or go electric. Home energy: efficient appliances, insulation, solar panels.

Diet: reduce meat, buy local, minimize waste. And advocacy: vote, speak up, support green policies.

Slide 12 ~1 min
The Paris Agreement Goals

The Paris Agreement set clear targets: limit warming to 1.5°C, achieve net-zero emissions by 2050, and provide $100 billion annually for developing nations.

As Obama said, "We are the first generation to feel climate change and the last that can do something about it."

Slide 13 ~1 min
Key Takeaways

To summarize: climate change is real and human-caused, effects are accelerating, impacts touch all life, solutions exist, and urgent action is needed.

Your next steps: calculate your carbon footprint, make one change today, stay informed, support good policies, and share this knowledge.

References
